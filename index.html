Мои курсы в Сколково

History and Philosophy of Automation

This course explored how scientific knowledge develops historically and how the way problems are framed shapes technological progress. We examined cases of “badly posed” questions in the history of science and learned how their reformulation enabled breakthroughs. The latter part focused on automation, analyzing its philosophical, cultural, and socio-economic implications, culminating in independent research projects and presentations.

Political Science

Throughout the module, we studied how political systems function and how power, institutions, and public policy influence decision-making. Using contemporary case studies, we analyzed elections, reforms, and conflicts of interest to understand how different actors — governments, businesses, and civil society — shape outcomes. The course strengthened our ability to interpret political environments from an applied perspective.

Psychology

The classes combined foundational social psychology with hands-on practice. We covered personality structure, communication patterns, group dynamics, and mechanisms of social influence, and then applied these concepts through experiments, case analyses, and self-reflection exercises. As a result, we developed tools for understanding behavior and working more effectively within teams.

Marketing

Learning was organized around solving real business challenges. We practiced market segmentation, positioning, brand strategy, and consumer research using both qualitative and quantitative methods. By collecting and interpreting data, we designed comprehensive marketing and communication strategies and presented them as full strategic plans.

Fundamentals of Sociology

This course provided the theoretical lens needed to interpret social reality systematically. Drawing on classical and contemporary sociological theories, we examined order, action, practices, culture, and the modern city. The main takeaway was the ability to choose an appropriate conceptual framework and use it to explain complex social phenomena.

City as Text

Structured as a field-based program, the course treated the city as a complex system that could be “read” through multiple disciplinary perspectives. Each day combined lectures with on-site research in small teams, followed by collective discussions and presentations. This immersive format developed interdisciplinary analysis skills and strengthened collaborative work.

Applied Sociology and Sociological Methods

Here we worked through the full research cycle from start to finish. Students formulated research problems, developed hypotheses, designed tools such as interview guides and surveys, built samples, and conducted both qualitative and quantitative data collection. The emphasis was on translating business or management issues into measurable social research questions and producing evidence-based insights.

World Culture: From the Paleolithic to the Present

The course offered a broad historical overview of global cultural development. Moving chronologically through major civilizations and artistic traditions, we studied styles, concepts, and analytical methods from cultural studies and art history. Practical exercises focused on interpreting and contextualizing cultural artifacts within their historical settings.

Biology of Human Behavior

The course explained behavior through biological mechanisms, integrating evolution, genetics, neurophysiology, and endocrinology. We examined how hormones, neurotransmitters, and environmental factors influence emotions, motivation, and social interaction. Analytical case discussions and AI-supported learning tools were used to connect theory with real-life examples.

Behavioral Economics

Rather than assuming perfect rationality, this course focused on systematic biases in decision-making. We studied heuristics, risk perception, strategic interaction, and intertemporal choice through experimental methods and economic models. Practical assignments included designing and running our own behavioral experiments and applying insights to management and business contexts.

Leadership Foundations

The emphasis here was on self-awareness and practical leadership skills. Through simulations, group exercises, and reflective discussions, we analyzed leadership styles, team dynamics, and ethical decision-making. Each participant concluded the course with a personalized leadership development plan.

Philosophy of Science

We examined what distinguishes scientific knowledge from other forms of inquiry and how theories evolve over time. Readings from Popper, Lakatos, Kuhn, and critical realism provided conceptual tools to discuss falsification, paradigms, and scientific revolutions. Historical case studies helped connect abstract philosophy to actual scientific practice.

Interdisciplinary Integration Workshop

This course functioned as a synthesis of the program. Working in teams, we combined approaches from sociology, psychology, management, and cultural analysis to address complex, real-world problems. The focus was on building coherent solutions by integrating multiple perspectives rather than relying on a single disciplinary method.
